CYBERSECURITY
==============

What is Cybersecurity?
Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, networks, and data from digital attacks, theft, and damage.

Common Cyber Threats:
- Malware: Software designed to damage or gain unauthorized access. Includes viruses, worms, trojans.
- Ransomware: Encrypts your files and demands payment to unlock them.
- Phishing: Fake emails or websites that trick you into giving personal information or passwords.
- Spyware: Software that secretly monitors your activity.
- DDoS Attack: Overwhelms a server with traffic to make it unavailable.
- Man-in-the-Middle Attack: Attacker intercepts communication between two parties.
- SQL Injection: Attacker inserts malicious code into a database query.
- Brute Force Attack: Tries thousands of password combinations to gain access.

Passwords Best Practices:
- Use at least 12 characters.
- Mix uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols.
- Never reuse passwords across sites.
- Use a password manager (e.g., Bitwarden, LastPass, 1Password).
- Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) everywhere possible.
- Never share passwords via email or chat.

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):
2FA adds an extra layer of security. Even if someone has your password, they also need a second factor (phone code, app, hardware key) to log in.

Antivirus and Antimalware:
- Keep antivirus software updated.
- Run regular scans.
- Popular options: Windows Defender (built-in), Malwarebytes, Bitdefender.

Safe Browsing:
- Always check for HTTPS (padlock icon) before entering sensitive info.
- Avoid clicking unknown links in emails.
- Do not download software from unofficial websites.
- Use a browser with built-in protection (Chrome, Firefox, Edge).
- Use an ad blocker to reduce malicious ad risks.

How to Spot a Phishing Email:
- Sender email address looks suspicious or misspelled.
- Urgent language: "Your account will be closed!"
- Asks for passwords, credit card numbers, or personal info.
- Links go to unusual URLs (hover over link to check).
- Poor grammar and spelling.

Data Encryption:
- Encryption converts data into unreadable format. Only authorized parties can decrypt it.
- HTTPS encrypts web traffic.
- Use BitLocker (Windows) or FileVault (Mac) to encrypt your hard drive.
- Encrypt sensitive files before sending them.

Firewall and Network Security:
- Always keep your firewall enabled.
- Use WPA3 or WPA2 encryption on your Wi-Fi network.
- Change default router username and password.
- Disable WPS (Wi-Fi Protected Setup) as it has vulnerabilities.
- Use a guest network for visitors and IoT devices.

Software Updates:
- Always install security updates promptly.
- Unpatched software is one of the most common ways attackers get in.
- Enable automatic updates for OS and applications.

Backup Strategy (3-2-1 Rule):
- 3 copies of your data
- 2 different storage types (e.g., external drive and cloud)
- 1 copy offsite (cloud or different location)

Incident Response - What to do if hacked:
1. Disconnect from the internet immediately.
2. Change all passwords from a different device.
3. Enable 2FA on all accounts.
4. Run a full antivirus scan.
5. Check for unauthorized account activity.
6. Report to your IT department or relevant authorities.
7. Restore from a clean backup if needed.
